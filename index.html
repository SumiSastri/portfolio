What makes me a happy developer

As a former Agile product-owner, I have been in tech for a while. A year ago, I made the big leap into the unknown to train as a full-stack developer at General Assembly.

It was one of the best decisions of my life. There is a great satisfaction building product features, working in a collaborative, supportive environment and learning from senior developers.

With one-year's experience, first as an intern at goodlord - a property-tech start-up, then freelancing with news publishers, the New Statesman, I looked for my first full-time job. I was one of 40 developers to be invited to Bletchley Park, by GVC, the sports gaming group, during the recruitment process. Our team of developers cracked the code and we won some swagger. After white-board tests and follow-up interviews, I was delighted to be one of two developers, to accept their offer.

I have learnt so much from all the teams I have worked with, as well as continuing to level-up my skills in my spare time. In the last year I have learnt TypeScript, React-Hooks, React-Redux. I have had a lot of enjoyment during the learning process. I have showcased the application of these new skills in my GitHub repo.
