What makes me a happy developer

As a former Agile product-owner, I have been in tech for a while. In January 2019, I made the big leap into the unknown to train as a full-stack developer at General Assembly.

It was one of the best decisions of my life. There is a great satisfaction building product features, working in a collaborative, supportive environment and learning from senior developers.

When I finished an internship at goodlord - a property-tech start-up, then freelancing with news publishers, the New Statesman, I looked for my first full-time job. I was one of 40 developers to be invited to Bletchley Park, by GVC, the sports gaming group, one of 2 to be offered a job and then one of 130 who lost jobs. The extraordinary year 2020, ended working on a contract with i6, a start-up in the airlines industry. It's been an eventful start to my new career and I have learnt so much from my peers and seniors.

I am currently working with Cancer Research UK as well as continuing to level-up my skills in my spare time. I enjoy the learning process and have showcased the application of these new skills in my GitHub repo.
